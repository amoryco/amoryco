Free the Beer - Supreme Court of Canada vs $292.50 Ticket

A New Brunswick man was ticketed by the RCMP after buying beer in Quebec and driving back to his home in New Brunswick. Things have escalated quickly and this case is now going to the Supreme Court of Canada.

How did a simple beer run turn into a constitutional kerfuffle?

After purchasing his beer in Quebec, this dangerous criminal was followed across provincial lines by the RCMP. How did they know he was carrying beer across provincial lines? Well, as the province puts it, “the RCMP had the defendant under surveillance”.

The RCMP probably didn’t take this offer

Mr. Comeau took his case to the provincial court and won. The judge found that the existing provincial liquor laws conflict with Section 121 of the Constitution Act, 1867.

Allow for me, a non expert on Canadian constitutional law to paraphrase - Section 121 can be summarized as “free trade between provinces”, and ticketing Canadians for purchasing goods from other provinces goes against the spirit and letter of Section 121.

Although this incident was caused by beer, this case could have far wider implications for extraprovincial trade. Yadda yadda yadda free the wine too.
